The Origin

I am a Bangladeshi high school researcher whose work lives at the collision point of artificial intelligence, theoretical physics, and the stubborn refusal to accept easy answers.

Not a straight path. An unusual one. From Khulna to global academic stages, I have built an AI ethics framework that got indexed on Google Scholar, won the Queen's Commonwealth Essay Gold Award beating 53,000+ writers across 56 nations, published independent research in physics on SSRN and PhilArchive, and shipped over 10 websites to the internet — all before finishing secondary school.

I co-author thermodynamics research with a Research Assistant Professor at the University of Oklahoma. I am a Lumiere Research Scholar with PhD-level mentors from Harvard, Stanford, and Oxford. I was selected for Stanford Code in Place from 66,000+ applicants and accepted into TKS — the global human accelerator.

My father died in 2024. He made me the man I am today. I have faced schizophrenia, bipolar disorder, and a life-threatening hospitalisation. My psychiatrist, Dr. Mst. Shammi Akhter, believed in me when I could barely believe in myself. Every framework I build about constraint and autonomy is, in some way, a love letter to the idea that structure does not cage you — it sets you free.

I am shaped by Orwell, Paulo Coelho, Humayun Ahmed. By Breaking Bad, Mr. Robot, Vinland Saga. By characters who walked through fire and came out changed, not destroyed.
